What we measure

Contradiction Density (CD)

Share of evaluated outputs that contain A vs ¬A conflicts under a reference oracle. Lower is better.

Contradiction Entropy (CE)

Disagreement across annotators/models. High CE means uncertainty clusters—useful for risk triage.

Coherence Delta (ΔC)

Gap between a model’s reasoning and its final answer. Large ΔC signals fragile explanations.

We combine disagreement-aware scoring, adversarial rounds, and heuristic stress tests into a single, reproducible risk score.

Compression-Aware Intelligence (CAI)

Definition: CAI is the practice of modeling intelligence as compression under constraints—systems are judged by how consistently they compress information without producing contradictions.

In CAI, contradictions are not noise; they are signals that compression failed or two constraints are at odds. Measuring those signals improves safety, clarity, and reliability.

Contradiction Compression

Many real problems are pairs of opposing demands (A, ¬A). Contradiction compression is the process of resolving those demands with minimal information loss. Contradish quantifies how well a system compresses contradictions across prompts, policies, and domains.

FAQ

Is this just another accuracy metric?

No. Accuracy plateaus hide instability. Contradiction metrics expose consistency and policy alignment—what enterprises and regulators actually need.

Why a neutral benchmark?

Self-reported scores lack credibility. External, reproducible scoring creates trust with stakeholders and customers.

Can we use our private data?

Yes. Audits can run locally or with strict deletion SLAs; public Index uses open test sets.
